Chole Bhature

Spiced chickpeas simmered in a smoky tomato-onion masala, served with puffed golden deep-fried bread.

4Servings
45 minCook
8 hrsSoak
MediumLevel
🫘
For the Chole
  • Dried chickpeas (kabuli chana)1½ cups
  • Onions, finely chopped2 large
  • Tomatoes, pureed3 medium
  • Ginger-garlic paste2 tbsp
  • Oil / ghee3 tbsp
  • Tea bags (for colour)2
🌶
Chole Spices
  • Chole masala powder2 tsp
  • Cumin seeds1 tsp
  • Coriander powder1½ tsp
  • Red chili powder1 tsp
  • Turmeric½ tsp
  • Amchur (dry mango powder)1 tsp
  • Bay leaves, cloves, black cardamom2, 3, 1
🫓
For the Bhature
  • All-purpose flour (maida)2 cups
  • Semolina (sooji)2 tbsp
  • Curd (yogurt)½ cup
  • Baking powder½ tsp
  • Sugar1 tsp
  • Oil (dough + deep fry)2 tbsp + for frying
🥗
To Serve
  • Pickled onions1 onion
  • Green chutneyas desired
  • Mango pickle (achaar)1–2 tsp
  • Fresh coriander + lemon wedgeshandful, 4
🫘 Cooking the Chole
1
Soak overnight. Rinse chickpeas and soak in plenty of water for at least 8 hours.
2
Pressure cook with tea. Cook with fresh water, tea bags, whole spices, and salt for 3–4 whistles until soft.
3
Build the masala. Heat oil, splutter cumin, sauté onions 10–12 min until deep golden brown.
4
Add aromatics & spices. Add ginger-garlic paste, tomato puree, cook until oil separates. Add all spice powders.
5
Simmer. Add chickpeas + stock. Simmer 15–20 min. Finish with amchur and fresh coriander.
🫓 Making the Bhature
1
Mix dough. Combine flour, sooji, baking powder, sugar, salt. Add curd and oil. Knead with warm water into a soft dough.
2
Rest 2 hours. Cover and rest at room temperature to ferment — this makes them puff dramatically.
3
Shape & fry. Roll into ovals on an oiled surface. Fry in hot oil (180°C) for 30–40 sec per side until golden.
4
Serve immediately with chole, pickled onions, chutney, and achaar.
Pro Tips
🍵

The Tea Trick

Tea bags give chickpeas dark colour without affecting flavour.

🧅

Patient Onions

Deep golden-brown onions are the flavour backbone — never rush them.

⏳

Rest the Dough

2-hour rest is non-negotiable for light, airy bhature.

🫙

Stock is Gold

Use chickpea cooking liquid in the gravy for deeper flavour.

🍋

Finish with Acid

Amchur or lemon at the end brightens all the spices perfectly.

Butter Chicken

Tender tandoori-style chicken in a velvety, mildly spiced tomato-butter-cream sauce — India's most beloved curry.

4Servings
50 minCook
2 hrsMarinate
MediumLevel
🍗
Chicken Marinade
  • Chicken (boneless thighs/breast)700g
  • Thick yogurt½ cup
  • Ginger-garlic paste1½ tbsp
  • Kashmiri chili powder1½ tsp
  • Garam masala½ tsp
  • Lemon juice + oil + salt1 tbsp, 1 tbsp, 1 tsp
🧅
Makhani Sauce
  • Butter3 tbsp
  • Onions, roughly chopped2 medium
  • Tomatoes, roughly chopped4 large
  • Cashews12–15
  • Ginger-garlic paste1½ tbsp
  • Green cardamom, cloves, bay leaf3, 3, 1
🌶
Sauce Spices & Finish
  • Kashmiri chili powder1½ tsp
  • Coriander + cumin powder1 tsp, ½ tsp
  • Garam masala1 tsp
  • Sugar1 tsp
  • Kasuri methi (dried fenugreek)1 tbsp
  • Heavy cream½ cup
🍗 Preparing the Chicken
1
Marinate. Mix all marinade ingredients with chicken. Refrigerate at least 2 hours (overnight is best).
2
Grill or pan-fry. Cook on a hot grill pan or oven at 220°C for 15–18 min until charred at the edges.
3
Rest and cut into bite-sized pieces. Set aside.
🧅 Making the Makhani Sauce
1
Cook base. In butter + oil, fry whole spices, then onions (8 min). Add ginger-garlic, then tomatoes and cashews. Cook covered 15 min until soft.
2
Blend smooth. Remove whole spices. Blend until silky, then pass through a sieve.
3
Cook sauce. Return to pan with butter, add spice powders, cook 3 min. Add grilled chicken, simmer 10 min.
4
Finish. Stir in cream, sugar, crushed kasuri methi. Simmer 3 min. Serve with naan or rice.
Pro Tips
🥛

Cashews = Creaminess

Cashews add natural body without heaviness — don't skip them.

🔥

Char the Chicken

Slight charring adds tandoor-like smokiness to the final dish.

🌿

Kasuri Methi Magic

Dried fenugreek is what gives butter chicken its signature aroma.

🧈

Cold Butter Finish

Swirl cold butter in just before serving for extra gloss and richness.

✦ Punjabi Soul Food · Dal

Dal Makhni

Slow-simmered black lentils and kidney beans enriched with butter and cream — the dal that ruins all other dals.

4Servings
1 hrCook
8 hrsSoak
EasyLevel
🫕
The Lentils
  • Whole black urad dal (sabut)1 cup
  • Red kidney beans (rajma)¼ cup
  • Salt1 tsp
🧅
Masala Base
  • Butter + oil3 tbsp + 1 tbsp
  • Onions, finely chopped2 medium
  • Tomatoes, pureed3 medium
  • Ginger-garlic paste2 tbsp
  • Green chili, slit1–2
  • Cumin seeds1 tsp
🌶
Spices & Finish
  • Kashmiri chili + coriander powder1 tsp, 1 tsp
  • Garam masala½ tsp
  • Heavy cream¼ cup
  • Butter (finish)2 tbsp
  • Kasuri methi1 tsp
🫕 Cooking the Dal
1
Soak overnight. Soak urad dal and rajma together in cold water for 8 hours. Drain and rinse.
2
Pressure cook. Cook with 4 cups water and 1 tsp salt for 6–8 whistles until completely soft and mushy.
3
Mash slightly. Mash some dal with a ladle — this naturally thickens the gravy.
🧅 The Makhni Masala
1
Temper. Heat butter + oil. Splutter cumin, sauté onions 10 min until golden.
2
Add ginger-garlic, chili, spice powders. Cook tomato puree until oil separates (8–10 min).
3
Slow simmer. Add cooked dal. Simmer on low 20–30 min, stirring often.
4
Finish rich. Stir in cream, butter, and kasuri methi. Simmer 5 more min. Serve with butter naan.
Pro Tips
⏳

Low & Slow

The longer you simmer, the creamier and more flavourful it becomes. 30 min minimum.

🧈

Butter is Key

It's literally in the name — use good quality salted butter.

🫙

Restaurant Secret

Restaurants simmer overnight on dum. Even a few extra hours dramatically deepens the flavour.

🌿

Crush Kasuri Methi

Rub between palms before adding to release maximum aroma.

✦ Hyderabadi Dum · Rice

Chicken Biryani

Fragrant basmati layered with marinated chicken, caramelised onions, saffron, and whole spices — sealed and slow-cooked on dum.

5Servings
90 minCook
3 hrsMarinate
HardLevel
🍗
Chicken Marinade
  • Chicken (bone-in pieces)1 kg
  • Thick yogurt¾ cup
  • Ginger-garlic paste2 tbsp
  • Red chili + biryani masala1½ tsp, 2 tsp
  • Turmeric + lemon juice½ tsp, 2 tbsp
  • Oil + salt2 tbsp, 1½ tsp
🍚
Rice & Whole Spices
  • Aged basmati rice3 cups
  • Bay leaves2
  • Green cardamom + cloves4, 4
  • Black peppercorns + star anise6, 1
  • Salt + oil2 tsp, 1 tbsp
🧅
Layering & Finish
  • Onions, thinly sliced (deep fried)3 large
  • Saffron in warm milkpinch in ¼ cup
  • Ghee3 tbsp
  • Fresh mint + corianderhandful each
  • Rose water (optional)1 tbsp
🍗 Marinate & Prep
1
Marinate chicken for 3 hours minimum (overnight best).
2
Make birista. Fry sliced onions in oil on medium heat until deep golden and crispy. Drain on paper towels.
3
Soak saffron in warm milk 15 min. Soak rice 30 min. Parboil rice with whole spices and salt until 70% cooked. Drain.
🍚 Dum Layering
1
Cook chicken in ghee on high 5 min, then medium 10 min until partially cooked.
2
Layer. Add half birista, half herbs over chicken. Spread parboiled rice on top. Add remaining birista, herbs, saffron milk, ghee, and rose water.
3
Dum cook. Seal lid with dough or foil. High heat 5 min, then lowest heat 25 min. Rest 10 min before opening.
4
Serve gently mixed with raita and salad.
Pro Tips
🍚

70% Cooked Rice

Never fully cook rice before layering — it finishes on dum and absorbs all flavours.

🧅

Birista is Everything

Perfectly crispy golden onions are the soul of biryani — don't rush this step.

🔒

Seal it Tight

A proper dum seal traps steam and flavour. Use dough or double foil under the lid.

🍖

Bone-in Chicken

Bone-in pieces stay juicier and contribute much more flavour to the rice.

Crispy Samosa

Flaky pastry triangles stuffed with spiced potato-pea filling — the eternal companion to masala chai.

12Pieces
60 minCook
30 minRest
MediumLevel
🥟
Pastry Shell
  • All-purpose flour (maida)2 cups
  • Ajwain (carom seeds)½ tsp
  • Salt½ tsp
  • Oil or ghee (moyan)4 tbsp
  • Cold water~½ cup
🥔
Potato Filling
  • Potatoes, boiled & mashed4 medium
  • Green peas (fresh/frozen)½ cup
  • Cumin seeds + oil1 tsp, 2 tbsp
  • Ginger, grated + green chili1 tsp, 2
  • Amchur + garam masala + coriander pwd1 tsp, ½ tsp, 1 tsp
  • Fresh coriander + salt2 tbsp, to taste
🥟 The Pastry
1
Make the dough. Rub oil into flour until it resembles breadcrumbs (this is moyan). Add ajwain and salt. Add cold water gradually and knead into a stiff dough.
2
Rest 30 min. Cover dough and rest for a flaky, crispy crust.
3
Shape cones. Roll into thin ovals, cut in half. Fold each semicircle into a cone and seal the seam with water.
🥔 Fill & Fry
1
Make filling. Heat oil, splutter cumin, add ginger and chili. Add peas 2 min. Add mashed potato and all spices. Mix and cool completely.
2
Fill & seal. Fill each cone, seal the open edge firmly with water. Press well to avoid bursting during frying.
3
Fry low & slow. Heat oil to 160°C. Fry on medium-low heat for 10–12 min, turning, until uniformly golden and crispy.
Pro Tips
❄️

Low Temperature

Fry on medium-low heat for a crispy, non-oily crust — high heat burns outside before inside heats.

🥔

Enough Moyan

Enough oil in the dough creates the characteristic flaky, layered texture.

💧

Dry Filling

Any moisture in the filling will make the pastry soggy and burst open during frying.

🔒

Seal Perfectly

Press the edge firmly — even a small leak floods the samosa with oil while frying.

Palak Paneer

Silky pureed spinach gravy with golden paneer cubes — vibrant, nutritious, and deeply comforting.

4Servings
35 minCook
EasyLevel
VegType
🥬
Spinach Base
  • Fresh spinach (palak)500g
  • Paneer, cubed250g
  • Green chili2
  • Garlic cloves + ginger4, 1 inch
🧅
Masala & Spices
  • Onion + tomatoes1 large, 2 medium
  • Butter/ghee + oil2 tbsp, 1 tbsp
  • Cumin seeds1 tsp
  • Red chili + coriander + garam masala½ tsp, 1 tsp, ½ tsp
  • Heavy cream3 tbsp
  • Saltto taste
🥬 Blanch & Blend Spinach
1
Blanch spinach. Boil in salted water 2 min. Immediately transfer to an ice bath to preserve the vibrant green colour.
2
Blend smooth with green chili, ginger, and garlic into a smooth puree. Set aside.
3
Fry paneer in butter until golden on the outside. Soak in warm salted water to keep soft. Set aside.
🧅 Build the Gravy
1
Temper. Heat butter + oil. Splutter cumin, sauté onions 8 min until golden.
2
Add tomatoes & spices. Cook tomato puree 5 min until oil separates. Add all spice powders, cook 1 min.
3
Add spinach. Pour in spinach puree. Simmer 5 min on medium heat.
4
Finish. Add paneer and cream. Simmer 3 min. Serve with naan or paratha.
Pro Tips
🧊

Ice Bath Secret

Shocking blanched spinach in ice water locks in brilliant green colour.

🧀

Soft Paneer

Soaking fried paneer in warm salted water keeps it pillowy soft.

🥛

Cream Control

Add cream off the heat or on very low to prevent curdling.

🍃

Fresh Spinach

Use fresh spinach — not frozen — for best colour and flavour.

Daal Baati Churma

Baked wheat dumplings dunked in five-lentil dal, served with sweet crumbled churma — the pride of Rajasthan.

4Servings
2 hrsCook
HardLevel
FestiveType
🫙
Baati (Dumplings)
  • Coarse wheat flour (atta)2 cups
  • Semolina (sooji)¼ cup
  • Ghee + salt + baking soda4 tbsp, ½ tsp, pinch
  • Warm water~¾ cup
  • Ghee (for dunking, heated)½ cup
🫕
Panchmel Dal
  • Chana + toor + moong + urad + masoor dal¼ cup each
  • Ghee + cumin + hing2 tbsp, 1 tsp, pinch
  • Red chili, turmeric, garam masala, amchur1 tsp each
  • Tomatoes + onion + ginger-garlic2, 1, 1 tbsp
🍯
Churma (Sweet)
  • Coarse wheat flour (atta)1½ cups
  • Ghee4 tbsp + for frying
  • Powdered sugar or jaggery½ cup
  • Cardamom powder½ tsp
  • Chopped almonds + pistachios2 tbsp
🫙 Baati
1
Make stiff dough. Mix flour, sooji, salt, baking soda. Rub in ghee until crumbly. Add warm water and knead into a very firm dough. Rest 20 min.
2
Shape. Divide into 12–14 portions, roll into smooth balls. Press a thumbprint dent into each to help cook through evenly.
3
Bake. Bake at 180°C for 30–35 min, turning once, until golden and hollow-sounding when tapped.
4
Ghee dunk. Break each hot baati in half and immerse in hot melted ghee for 2–3 min. This is the soul of the dish.
🫕 Dal & 🍯 Churma
1
Pressure cook all five dals with water, salt, and turmeric for 4 whistles.
2
Temper dal. Heat ghee, add cumin and hing, fry onions, ginger-garlic, tomatoes, and spices. Mix into cooked dal. Simmer 10 min.
3
Make churma. Mix flour and ghee into a stiff dough, shape into a disc. Deep fry on low heat until golden throughout. Cool completely.
4
Finish churma. Crumble the fried disc. Mix in powdered sugar, cardamom, nuts, and a drizzle of ghee. Serve the trio together.
Pro Tips
🧈

Ghee Dunk = Non-Negotiable

Dunking hot baati in ghee is what transforms them into the legendary dish they are.

🔥

Traditional Fire

Traditionally baked over charcoal. A grill or oven works — but charcoal adds smokiness.

🥜

Stiff Dough

Much stiffer than chapati dough — essential for the dense, crumbly baati texture.

🍯

Churma Timing

Make churma last so it stays warm and fragrant when you serve the trio together.

Gulab Jamun

Soft, spongy milk-solid dumplings fried golden and soaked in a fragrant rose-cardamom sugar syrup.

20Pieces
40 minCook
2 hrsSoak
MediumLevel
🍮
Jamun Dough
  • Khoya (mawa) / milk powder1 cup
  • All-purpose flour (maida)3 tbsp
  • Baking sodaa tiny pinch
  • Cardamom powder¼ tsp
  • Warm milk (to bind)2–3 tbsp
  • Ghee or oil (for frying)for deep frying
🌹
Rose Sugar Syrup
  • Sugar2 cups
  • Water1½ cups
  • Green cardamom, crushed4
  • Rose water1 tbsp
  • Saffron strandsa pinch
  • Lemon juice½ tsp
🌹 Sugar Syrup First
1
Make syrup. Combine sugar, water, and cardamom. Boil, stirring until sugar dissolves. Simmer 5 min until slightly sticky (1-string consistency).
2
Add fragrance. Stir in rose water, saffron, and lemon juice. Keep warm on the lowest heat.
🍮 Fry the Jamuns
1
Make dough. Crumble khoya smoothly. Mix in flour, baking soda, and cardamom. Add just enough warm milk to form a soft, non-sticky dough. Do not over-knead.
2
Shape. Roll into 20 smooth, crack-free balls. Any crack will cause them to split during frying.
3
Fry low & slow. Heat ghee to 140–150°C (very low-medium). Fry 8–10 min, gently rolling, until deep mahogany brown all over.
4
Soak. Drop hot jamuns into warm syrup. Soak minimum 2 hours. They become softer and more flavourful with time.
Pro Tips
🌡️

Very Low Oil Temp

The biggest mistake is hot oil. Low heat ensures inside cooks through before outside burns.

🔮

Crack-Free Balls

Take time to roll each jamun perfectly smooth — any crack means it bursts in oil.

🌡️

Warm Syrup

Add jamuns to warm (not boiling) syrup so they absorb it evenly without hardening.

⏳

Patience Pays

Jamuns soaked overnight are exponentially better than after just 2 hours. Make ahead!

Tonkotsu Ramen

Rich, milky pork bone broth with chewy noodles, melt-in-mouth chashu pork, a jammy marinated egg, bamboo shoots, and nori.

4Servings
3 hrsCook
HardLevel
JapaneseOrigin
🦴
Tonkotsu Broth
  • Pork trotters / neck bones1 kg
  • Water3 litres
  • Garlic + ginger + green onion6, 6 slices, 3 stalks
  • Dried shiitake + kombu4, 1 piece
  • Soy sauce + sesame oil2 tbsp, 1 tbsp
  • Salt + white pepperto taste
🥩
Chashu Pork
  • Pork belly, skin-on600g
  • Soy sauce + mirin + sake½ cup, ¼ cup, ¼ cup
  • Sugar + water2 tbsp, ¼ cup
  • Garlic (smashed) + ginger3 cloves, 3 slices
🥚
Noodles & Toppings
  • Fresh or dried ramen noodles400g
  • Eggs (for soft boiled)4
  • Soy sauce + mirin (egg marinade)3 tbsp, 1 tbsp
  • Bamboo shoots (menma)½ cup
  • Nori + green onions + corn4 half-sheets, 4, ½ cup
  • Sesame seeds + chili oil1 tbsp, to taste
🦴 The Tonkotsu Broth
1
Blanch bones. Boil bones 10 min. Drain and rinse under cold water to remove impurities for a clean, flavourful broth.
2
Build broth. Add cleaned bones to 3L fresh water with garlic, ginger, green onions, mushrooms, and kombu.
3
Hard boil 2 hours. Keep on a vigorous boil (not simmer). The rolling boil emulsifies collagen into the characteristic milky white broth.
4
Season. Strain, then season with soy sauce, sesame oil, salt, and white pepper. Keep warm.
🥩 Chashu & Assembly
1
Roll & sear pork belly. Roll tightly, tie with twine. Sear all sides in a hot pan until browned.
2
Braise chashu. Add soy, mirin, sake, sugar, water, garlic, ginger. Simmer covered on low 1.5 hours, turning every 30 min. Cool in liquid, then slice.
3
Marinated eggs. Boil eggs exactly 6.5 min. Peel and marinate in soy + mirin for at least 4 hours.
4
Cook noodles per package instructions. Divide into pre-heated deep bowls.
5
Assemble. Pour hot broth. Top with sliced chashu, halved egg, bamboo shoots, corn, nori, green onions, sesame seeds, and chili oil.
Pro Tips
🌊

Vigorous Boil

Tonkotsu needs to boil hard, not simmer — the vigorous boil creates its iconic milky colour.

🥚

Jammy Egg Timing

Exactly 6.5 min in boiling water, then ice bath, for the perfect custardy golden yolk.

🍜

Pre-Heat Bowls

Fill bowls with boiling water before serving — ramen cools down very fast.

🥩

Cool Chashu in Liquid

Always cool the pork belly in its braising liquid for maximum moisture and flavour.

🧂

Tare is Key

Use the chashu braising liquid as your "tare" — add a spoonful to each bowl for depth.
